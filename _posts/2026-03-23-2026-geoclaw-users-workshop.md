More than **70+ attendees** throughout two workshop days contributed to a very successful [GeoClaw User's Workshop](https://www.clawpack.org/geoclaw-users-2026), which took place online on March 19-20, 2026. Following the description from the [workshop's webpage](https://www.clawpack.org/geoclaw-users-2026/#workshop-description), the event featured "a series of short talks by developers and users about interesting [GeoClaw](https://www.geoclaw.org)-related projects (including also related packages such as [D-Claw](https://www.clawpack.org/dclaw.html), [ForestClaw](http://www.forestclaw.org/ForestClaw/index.html), [BingClaw](https://github.com/norwegian-geotechnical-institute/BingCLAW_5.6.1), etc.) interspersed, with time for discussion and break-out groups to enable chatting more with the speakers or other participants." All speakers are listed in the online [schedule](https://www.clawpack.org/geoclaw-users-2026/schedule), with individual speaker pages for some of them.

Most of the talks were recorded and are available on YouTube (see [Clawpack Youtube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@clawpackvideos3679/videos)), and so was mine, entitled "**From Multi-CMT to Dynamic Rupture Models: Informing GeoClaw Tsunami Simulations With Increasing Source Complexity**".
